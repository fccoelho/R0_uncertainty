 \section*{Alternative formulations for SIR Models}
 
 Assuming the typical SIR formulation as 3 ordinary differential equations on a 
closed population: $S(t)+I(t)+R(t)=N$, $\forall t \geq 0$. We have that for 
this formulation the basic reproduction number ${\cal 
R}_0=\frac{\beta}{\gamma}$.

If we take the ratio:
\begin{equation}
 \frac{dS}{dR} = \frac{-\beta S I}{\gamma I}=-{\cal R}_0 S
\end{equation}

It can be integrated to $S(t)=S_0 e^{-{\cal R}_0 R}$. We can then substitute 
this definition of $S(t)$ into the standard $\frac{dR}{dt}=\gamma I$, and obtain

\begin{equation}
 \frac{dR}{dt}=\gamma\left(N-R-S_0 e^{-{\cal R}_0 R} \right)
 \label{eq:R}
\end{equation}


With this we have reduce a 3-dimensional system to a single equation embodying 
the full SIR dynamics.

\section{Approximate Solution}
Equation \ref{eq:R} does not have a direct solution. but an approximate solution can be obtained by assuming ${\cal R}_0 R$ remains small for then $e^{-{\cal R}_0 R} \approxeq 1-({\cal R}_0 R})+({\cal R}_0 R})^2$ and (\ref{eq:R}) reduces to the first order quadratic ODE

\begin{equation}
\frac{dZ}{dt}\approxeq \gamma\left(N-S_0 +[S_0 {\cal R}_0 - 1]R-(S_0{\cal R}_0^2/2)R^2 \right)
\end{equation}

which takes the standard solution

\begin{equation}
 R(t)=\frac{1}{{\cal R}_0^2S_0} \{(S_0{\cal R}_0)-1+\alpha \tanh[(\alpha\gamma t/2)-\phi]}
\end{equation}
where the amplitude $\alpha=\sqrt{[S_0{\cal R}_0 - 1]^2 + 2S_0I_0{\cal R}_0^2}$, and the phase $\phi=\tanh^{-1}\{(S_0{\cal R}_0 -1)/\alpha\}$
